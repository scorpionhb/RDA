RDA's History
The organisation was formed in 1965 called the Advisory Council on Riding for the Disabled and had 9 member groups. By 1969, it adopted its current moniker and had grown to a membership of 80 groups. Today, the association has 500 member groups representing over 25,000 participants and 18,000 volunteers.
About RDA
Riding for the Disabled Association Incorporating Carriage Driving (RDA) is dedicated to improving the lives of people with disabilities through the provision of horse riding and carriage driving.
RDA is a member of the British Equestrian Federation (BEF). RDA is a charity registered in England and Wales (No: 244108) and Scotland (No: SC039473).
RDA's Mission
Through a network of over 500 volunteer groups throughout the UK, RDA provides opportunities for therapy, achievement and enjoyment, improving health, wellbeing and self-confidence, and benefiting mobility and co-ordination.
